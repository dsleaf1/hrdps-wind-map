Reading the rings — a plain-language guide

Each ring shows how rough the water is likely to get where the tidal current pushes against the waves — the classic "wind against tide" chop that steepens and breaks. It's driven by the current, so scrub the timeline: a pass can be glassy at slack and light up as the ebb or flood builds against the sea.

  • Green — gentle; go paddle.
  • Yellow — whitecaps building, a bit lumpy.
  • Orange — steep, breaking crests; committing water.
  • Red — very steep, lots of breaking.
  • Dark red — tide race / overfalls: steep breaking standing waves. Avoid, or portage the plan.

Rings are most trustworthy at exposed spots; deep inside the islands the wave forecast is coarse, so treat green there as "no strong signal," not a guarantee. When in doubt, plan to cross a pass near slack water.
